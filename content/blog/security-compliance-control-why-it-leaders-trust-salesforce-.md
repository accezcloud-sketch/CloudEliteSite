### The Problem
The most pressing concern for any IT leader is the protection of sensitive customer data, intellectual property, and mission-critical business information. Data breaches, whether accidental or malicious, can lead to catastrophic financial losses, severe reputational damage, and a fundamental erosion of customer trust. In a region like Saudi Arabia, where rapid economic growth and digital adoption are paramount, the stakes are even higher, as the reputation of businesses directly impacts their ability to attract investment and talent.

### Why It Happens
Traditional on-premises solutions often suffer from outdated security protocols, a lack of continuous patching and updating, and a limited capacity to scale security measures in line with evolving threats. Furthermore, the complexity of managing security across disparate systems and the constant need for skilled security personnel create significant operational burdens and introduce human error. The rise of sophisticated cyberattacks, including ransomware, phishing, and insider threats, means that even well-intentioned organizations can fall victim if their security infrastructure is not robust and proactive.

The sheer volume and complexity of regulations, coupled with the global nature of data flows, make it challenging for businesses to stay on top of their obligations across all their systems.
